1. General Overview

> Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and the third-largest in diameter in the solar system.
> Named after the Greek god of the sky.
> Diameter: ~50,724 km (about 4 times the size of Earth).

2. Orbit and Rotation

> Uranus takes ~84 Earth years to complete one orbit around the Sun.
> A day on Uranus lasts ~17.2 hours.
> Its axis is tilted at an extreme 98°, causing it to rotate on its side.

3. Surface and Composition

> Uranus is an ice giant composed of hydrogen, helium, and "ices" like water, methane, and ammonia.
> No solid surface; the visible layer is composed of clouds.
> The planet’s bluish color is due to methane in its atmosphere absorbing red light.

4. Atmosphere and Temperature

> Atmosphere is mostly hydrogen (~83%) and helium (~15%), with traces of methane.
> Coldest planet in the solar system with average temperatures of ~-224°C (-371°F).
> Wind speeds can reach up to 900 km/h (560 mph).

5. Unique Facts

> Uranus has 27 known moons, including Titania, Oberon, and Miranda.
> It has faint rings composed of dark particles.
> Discovered by William Herschel in 1781, making it the first planet discovered with a telescope.
