1. General Overview

> Pluto is a dwarf planet in the Kuiper Belt, a region of icy bodies beyond Neptune.
> Named after the Roman god of the underworld.
> Diameter: ~2,377 km (about 18.5% the size of Earth).

2. Orbit and Rotation

> Pluto takes ~248 Earth years to complete one orbit around the Sun.
> A day on Pluto (Plutonian day) lasts ~6.4 Earth days.
> Its orbit is highly elliptical, sometimes bringing it closer to the Sun than Neptune.

3. Surface and Composition

> Pluto's surface is composed of nitrogen ice, with regions of methane and carbon monoxide ice.
> Key features: Sputnik Planitia, a vast, heart-shaped glacier.
> Its reddish-brown color comes from tholins, organic compounds formed by solar radiation.

4. Atmosphere and Temperature

> Pluto has a thin atmosphere primarily made of nitrogen, with traces of methane and carbon monoxide.
> Average surface temperature: ~-229°C (-380°F).
> The atmosphere expands and collapses as Pluto orbits closer to or farther from the Sun.

5. Unique Facts

> Pluto has five known moons, with Charon being the largest and almost half the size of Pluto.
> It was reclassified as a dwarf planet in 2006 by the International Astronomical Union.
> Discovered in 1930 by Clyde Tombaugh.
