1. General Overview

> Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest in our solar system.
> Named after the king of the Roman gods.
> Diameter: ~139,820 km (11 times the size of Earth).

2. Orbit and Rotation

> Jupiter takes ~12 Earth years to complete one orbit around the Sun.
> A day on Jupiter lasts only ~9.9 hours, the shortest of all planets.
> Axis tilt of 3° means it has minimal seasonal variation.

3. Surface and Composition

> Jupiter is a gas giant with no solid surface; primarily composed of hydrogen and helium.
> Features the Great Red Spot, a massive storm larger than Earth that has lasted for centuries.
> The planet's core is thought to be rocky, surrounded by a thick layer of metallic hydrogen.

4. Atmosphere and Temperature

> Atmosphere is made of ~90% hydrogen and ~10% helium, with traces of methane, ammonia, and water vapor.
> Average temperature at cloud tops: ~-145°C (-234°F).
> Displays colorful cloud bands caused by strong winds and chemical composition.

5. Unique Facts

> Jupiter has 95 known moons, including the four Galilean moons: Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto.
> Ganymede is the largest moon in the solar system, even bigger than Mercury.
> The planet has a faint ring system composed of dust particles.
