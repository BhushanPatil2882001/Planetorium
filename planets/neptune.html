1. General Overview

> Neptune is the eighth and farthest planet from the Sun in our solar system.
> Named after the Roman god of the sea.
> Diameter: ~49,244 km (about 3.9 times the size of Earth).

2. Orbit and Rotation

> Neptune takes ~165 Earth years to complete one orbit around the Sun.
> A day on Neptune lasts ~16.1 hours.
> Its orbit is nearly circular and the farthest from the Sun.

3. Surface and Composition

> Neptune is an ice giant composed of hydrogen, helium, and methane.
> The planet has no solid surface, with its visible features being layers of clouds.
> Methane in the atmosphere gives Neptune its deep blue color.

4. Atmosphere and Temperature

> Atmosphere is primarily hydrogen (~80%) and helium (~19%), with traces of methane.
> Average temperature: ~-214°C (-353°F).
> Neptune experiences the fastest winds in the solar system, reaching up to 2,100 km/h (1,300 mph).

5. Unique Facts

> Neptune has 14 known moons, with Triton being the largest and only large moon in the solar system with a retrograde orbit.
> It has faint rings composed of ice particles and dust.
> Discovered in 1846, Neptune was the first planet located through mathematical predictions.
